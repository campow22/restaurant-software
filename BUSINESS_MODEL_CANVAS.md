# Business Model Canvas - Restaurant Training Platform

## Customer Segments

**Primary Target: Fine Dining Restaurants**
- Characteristics:
  - Complex menus with 50+ items
  - Sophisticated wine/beverage programs
  - High service standards and expectations
  - Price points justify investment in staff quality
  - Lower staff volume but higher individual impact
  - Turnover costs are significant (3-6 months training typical)

**Early Adopter Profile:**
- Restaurant groups with 2-5 fine dining locations
- Progressive owners who value technology
- Currently struggling with inconsistent training across locations
- Experiencing high turnover in server positions
- Want to promote from within but lack structured pathways

## Value Propositions

**For Restaurant Management (Buyer):**
- **Almost zero setup effort** - AI generates training content automatically from existing menus
- Improve service quality and consistency across staff
- Reduce costly errors (wrong orders, allergen mistakes, comp situations)
- Increase sales revenue through better upselling and menu knowledge
- Reduce turnover costs via faster, more effective training (compress 3-6 months to 4-8 weeks)
- Create promotion pathways from busser → server → lead server
- Real-time visibility into staff menu knowledge and training gaps
- Quantifiable ROI through error reduction and sales lift

**For Servers & Support Staff (End User):**
- Confidence in guest interactions through comprehensive menu knowledge
- Increased sales ability → higher tips and income
- Clear, achievable path to advance from support roles to serving
- Reduced anxiety during shifts
- Improved job satisfaction and professional development
- Gamified learning that's engaging vs. traditional training manuals

## Channels

**Go-to-Market Strategy (Year 1):**

1. **Direct Outreach (Primary - Months 1-6)**
   - LinkedIn targeting restaurant owners/GMs in major metros
   - Personal network activation
   - Cold email to restaurants meeting profile
   - Goal: 30-50 problem validation interviews

2. **Restaurant Industry Events (Secondary - Ongoing)**
   - National Restaurant Association Show
   - Regional restaurant association meetings
   - Local hospitality networking events
   - Goal: 5-10 events in Year 1, generate leads and credibility

3. **Future Channels (Post-validation):**
   - Content marketing (restaurant management blog)
   - Partnerships with POS systems
   - Referrals from successful customers
   - Trade publication advertising

## Customer Relationships

**Relationship Type:** High-touch, consultative in early stages

- **Problem Validation Phase:** Deep interviews, understanding pain points
- **Beta/Pilot Phase:** Weekly check-ins, co-creation of features
- **Early Customers:** Dedicated support, quarterly business reviews
- **Scale Phase:** Self-service onboarding, tiered support, community

**Success Metrics to Track:**
- Restaurant: Error rates, sales per server, training time to proficiency, turnover rates
- Servers: Quiz scores, module completion, time to certification, engagement frequency

## Revenue Streams

**Primary Model: Per-User Monthly Subscription**

**Pricing Structure (Initial Hypothesis - TO BE VALIDATED):**
- $15-25 per active user per month
- Minimum 10 users per restaurant
- Annual commitment discount (15-20%)

**Example Restaurant Economics:**
- Fine dining restaurant: 20 servers + 10 support staff
- $20/user × 30 users = $600/month or $7,200/year
- Target: $10K-15K annual contract value per location

**Pricing Validation Questions:**
- Is this less than one prevented error per month?
- Is this less than avoiding one turnover event per year?
- What % of current training budget does this represent?

**Future Revenue Opportunities:**
- Premium features (advanced analytics, custom content)
- Professional services (custom training program design)
- White label for restaurant groups
- Content marketplace for industry experts

## Key Activities

**Phase 1: Problem Validation (Current Phase - Months 1-2)**
- Conduct 30-50 customer discovery interviews
- Map current training processes and pain points
- Validate willingness to pay and price sensitivity
- Identify key metrics restaurants care about

**Phase 2: MVP Development (Months 3-4)**
- Build menu knowledge quiz platform
- Focus on simplest viable solution
- Manual content creation initially (before AI automation)
- Manager dashboard showing staff knowledge gaps

**Phase 3: Solution Validation (Months 5-6)**
- 2-3 pilot restaurants using MVP
- Weekly iteration based on feedback
- Measure engagement, completion rates, satisfaction
- Validate that tool improves measurable outcomes

**Phase 4: Scale Preparation (Months 7-12)**
- Build AI content generation capability
- Refine based on pilot learnings
- Develop sales process and materials
- Prepare for customer acquisition

**Ongoing:**
- Customer success and iteration (highest priority)
- Customer interviews and feedback loops
- Metric tracking and hypothesis testing

## Key Partnerships

**Priority Partnerships:**

1. **Early Stage (Validation):**

2. **Growth Stage:**

## Cost Structure

**Lean Startup Approach - Minimize Fixed Costs**

**Development Phase:**
- Founder time (opportunity cost)
- Basic tech infrastructure (hosting, domains, tools): $100-500/month
- Travel to customer interviews and events: Variable
- Industry event attendance: $1K-5K per event
- Prototype development: Time investment primarily

**Post-MVP Phase:**
- Cloud infrastructure (AWS/GCP): Variable with usage
- AI/ML API costs (OpenAI, etc.): Variable with usage
- Customer acquisition cost: TBD based on channel effectiveness
- Customer success/support time
- Sales and marketing expenses

**Key Cost Principles:**
- Minimize burn rate during validation
- Invest heavily in customer learning, not premature scaling
- Use existing tools and platforms where possible
- Founder-led activities for everything in early stages

## Unfair Advantage

**AI-Powered Content Generation**

The core differentiator is eliminating setup friction:
- Restaurant uploads menu → AI generates training content automatically
- Reduces setup from weeks/months to hours/days
- This is defensible if execution is excellent and you move fast
- Network effects: more restaurants → better AI training → better content

**Secondary Advantages:**
- First-mover in fine dining segment
- Deep customer relationships from high-touch early phase
- Data on what training content drives outcomes
- Restaurant industry expertise and connections

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Product-Market Fit is everything** - Don't build until you validate the problem deeply
2. **Pricing must reflect ROI** - Can quantify value in error reduction, sales lift, turnover savings
3. **Adoption is critical** - Staff must actually use it or it fails regardless of quality
4. **AI must deliver on promise** - Content generation is the differentiator; it must work well
5. **Measure outcomes** - Need proof that training improves performance metrics
